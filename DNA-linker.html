Protocol

DNA Origami Linker

14
Feb

Our dirty lab work...shh!

Last updated 25.09.16

Materials needed:

  • Cryo-vial
  • Glass pipette tips
  • Argon or nitrogen gas
  • Lipid 1: DOPC (1,2-dioleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine)
  • Lipid 2: biotinylated DPPE (1,2 Dipalmitoyl-sn-Glycero-3-Phosphoethanolamine-N-(CapBiotinyl)(Sodium Salt))
  • Liquid nitrogen
  • Vaccum oven
  • Syringes (1.5 ml)
  • Suitable buffer (1x PBS)
  • Sonicator
  • Extrusion kit (polycarbonate membrane, filter supports)
  • 70% Ethanol

Procedure:

  1. Take 1 mg/ml of 10 mg/ml of DOPC into a cryo-vial (safe for liquid nitrogen) with the help of a glass pipette tip (due to presence of chloroform). (A regular pipette tip can also be used with a quick motion)
  2. For evaporation of chloroform, use a very gentle flow of argon gas (if not available, use nitrogen gas). With the help of a nozzle, rotate the vial continuously to get a homogeneous layer of lipids (lipid cake)
  3. Oxygen should not be used to dry in case of unsaturated chains as they might get hydrolyzed.
  4. Add the vial to a vacuum oven at room temperature and leave it for 10-15 minutes
  5. Fill the small can with liquid nitrogen till the top
  6. Get 1.5 ml of buffer so that the concentration would become 2 mg/ml. Normally he uses 5 mg/ml
  7. Sonicate the solution for ~5 minutes, freeze and sonicate again 3 times
  8. In the meantime, put together the extrusion kit (Avanti lipids Fig 1):
    • Put 2 filter supports (wet them so they stick together) on each side of the extrusion set
    • Use Avastin polycarbonate membrane and place between the 4 filter supports
    • Fit the two parts together with the protector ring and donâ€™t close too tightly

  9. Take the two 1 ml syringes (one for up and one for down). Pull the buffer in one, and remove all air bubbles
  10. Fit the two syringes on either side and puch the buffer out from one side so that it fills into the other syringe. Remove bubbles from the second syringe and repeat. Check for leaks
  11. Now discard the buffer and take the lipid solution repeat 20+1 times to get the vesicles into the second syringe
  12. Store in fridge
  13. Put the remaining liquid nitrogen into the container
  14. Syringe: fill and clean with ethanol 3 times
  15. Clean with water
  16. Blow dry them with nitrogen

Results:

Vesicles were obtained with the sizes 198-189 nm as measured with the DLS (Dynamic Light Scattering)
