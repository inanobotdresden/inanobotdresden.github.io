Protocol

Liposome Formation

14
Feb

Our dirty lab work...shh!

Last updated 25.09.16

Materials needed:
Reactive amino group with 9-fluorenylmethoxycarbonyl (Fmoc)
2-(1H-benzotriazol-1-yl)-1,1,3,3-tetramethyluronoium hexafluorphosphate (HBTU)
Acetic Anhydride
O-(7-Azabenzotriazol-1-yl)-N,N,N′,N′-tetramethyluronium-hexafluorphosphat (HATU)
1-Hydroxy-7-azabenzotriazol (HOAT)
Diisopropylethylamin (DIPEA)
TFA/TIS/water/DTT (90v/v):5(v/v):2.5(v/v):2.5(m/v))
Mili-Q water/acetonitrile (1:1)

Procedure:
The peptides polyR and Transportan were synthesized on the basis of standard Fmoc solid-phase peptide synthesis chemistry. All peptides were synthesized with the help of an automated solid-phase peptide synthesizer (ResPep SL, Intavis) on the basis of standard Fmoc solid-phase peptide synthesis chemistry. This synthesis strategy allows the coupling of only one amino acid per cycle by protecting the reactive amino group with 9-fluorenylmethoxycarbonyl (Fmoc). For this reason, deprotection is required at the beginning of each cycle. The activation of the carboxy group, which enables the coupling of the next amino acid was achieved by 2-(1H-benzotriazol-1-yl)-1,1,3,3-tetramethyluronoium hexafluorphosphate (HBTU). Thus each cycle consists consecutively of the steps deprotection, activation and coupling. In order to ensure maximal sequence specificity, each amino acid was coupled twice with 5 times excess and amino acids that did not react were caped with acetic anhydride

Following the final cycle, the resin bound peptides were biotinylated manually via O-(7-Azabenzotriazol-1-yl)-N,N,N′,N′-tetramethyluronium-hexafluorphosphat (HATU), 1-Hydroxy-7-azabenzotriazol (HOAT) and Diisopropylethylamin (DIPEA) mediated coupling and cleaved from the resin with TFA/TIS/water/DTT (90v/v):5(v/v):2.5(v/v):2.5(m/v)) for 2 hours. The precipitation of the final peptide was performed and additionally washed with ice-cold diethyl ether. The precipitated peptide was solved in Mili-Q water/acetonitrile (1:1) and further purified using a reverse-phase high pressure liquid chromatography (HPLC) on a Semi-preparative HPLC (Waters) equipped with a semi-preparative C18 column, (Phenomenex). During the purification, the peptide attaches to the solid phase of the column and is then eluted by the mobile phase over 25 min at 4 mL/min according to its hydrophobic profile. Therefore, the mobile phase is represented by a gradient of 0.1% TFA in water (100% to 5%) and 0.1% TFA in acetontrile (5% to 100%). The fraction containing the product can be identified with the help of an UV detector at 220nm. For further analysis, the fraction containing the product was submitted to analytical reverse phase Ultra-high Pressure Liquid Chromatography (UPLC Aquity with UV Detector) equipped with an analytical C18 column (ACQUITY UPLC BEH C18, bead size 1.7 µm, 50x2.1 mm), that uses an anisocratic gradient and is directly connected to an electrospray ionization mass spectrometer (ESI-MS, ACQUITY TQ Detector). Therefore, sequence specificity can be confirmed on the base of the molecular weight. Finally, the fraction containing the product was lyophilized and stored at -20°C.
